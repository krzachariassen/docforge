# Strategy Document Template

For long-form strategic documents: technology bets, platform investments, multi-team initiatives, org restructuring proposals.

**Audience**: Senior engineers and leadership. They want specificity, evidence, and clear asks. They push back on vague claims and missing failure modes.

---

## Sections

### Executive Summary
2–3 paragraphs. Core argument, proposed approach, and ask — for someone who only reads this section. Write it last, place it first.

### Problem Statement / Why
What problem, and why now? Current state costs (quantified). Why hasn't this been solved. Why now is the right time.

### Current State
Concrete description of where things stand. Specific systems, failure modes, numbers. No vague language.

### Proposed Approach / What
What are we proposing? High-level direction — clear enough that a skeptic can disagree with it.

### Architecture / How
The technical or organizational design. Specific enough to be buildable. Include system interactions and ownership.

### Roadmap / Phases
Work broken into phases with milestones. Each phase: what gets built, dependencies, definition of done.

### Measurement / Success Criteria
How will we know this worked? Leading indicators (early signals). Lagging indicators (outcomes). What failure looks like.

### Risks & Mitigations
For each major risk: what it is, probability and impact (H/M/L), mitigation strategy, residual risk.

### Resource Requirements
Engineering headcount (teams, sizes, duration). Infrastructure costs. External dependencies.

### Decision Points
What decisions does leadership need to make? Be explicit — a document that leaves readers unsure what they're approving has failed.

### Getting Started
The concrete first step. If approved, what happens on Monday?
