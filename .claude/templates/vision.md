# Vision Document Template

For high-altitude vision documents: product north stars, platform futures, organizational direction, multi-year technology direction.

**Audience**: Leadership and cross-functional partners. They want inspiration grounded in reality. They distrust vague promises. The best vision documents make a specific, bounded bet and explain why it's the right one.

---

## Sections

### Executive Summary
The vision in 2–3 paragraphs. What world are we building toward, and why does it matter? Write last, place first.

### Why This Matters Now
What is changing — in the market, the technology landscape, or the organization — that makes this vision timely? A vision without a "why now" is just aspiration.

### North Star
One clear statement of what success looks like in 3–5 years. Specific enough to be falsifiable. "By [year], [specific capability] will enable [specific outcome] for [specific audience]."

### What This Means
What capabilities, behaviors, or structures need to exist for the North Star to be real? Translate the vision into concrete terms.

### What We Give Back
What does achieving this enable for users, customers, or the organization? The human case for why this matters.

### What Success Looks Like
3–5 measurable indicators that the vision is being realized. No vanity metrics.

### Operating Principles
3–5 principles that guide decisions. Each must create a real tension: "We choose X over Y when they conflict." Principles without trade-offs are platitudes.

### Milestones
Key checkpoints on the path to the North Star. Observable and time-bound. What can we point to at 6 months, 1 year, 2 years?

### Leadership Ask
What are you asking leadership to do, decide, or resource? A vision that asks for nothing goes nowhere.
